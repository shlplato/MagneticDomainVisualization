Description

Magnetism is a size dependent property. At the macroscale, ferromagnetic materials exhibit remanance – when magnetized, they can stay magnetized.

At the nanoscale, ferromagnetic materials behave paramagnetically. They are magnetized in an applied field, but when the field is removed, they spontaneously de-magnetize. This simulation allows students to investigate the effects of particle size, applied field, temperature and the probability of a ferromagnetic particle staying magnetized.

Legend
Arrows – the magnetic orientation (magnetic moment) of an individual atom
Shading – indicates probability that the magnetic moment will flip
Domain size – the size of the particle
Controlled by the slider or keys A / S
Applied field – strength of the field applied to the particle
Controlled by the slider or keys G / H
Temperature – thermal energy of the domain
Controlled by the slider or keys K / L
